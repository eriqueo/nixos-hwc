### What Is the Largest Deck Without a Permit?

In Bozeman, a deck generally does not require a permit if it is **less than 30 inches above grade** and attached to a single-family home. This usually means ground-level or low-profile decks. There is no strict maximum square footage at the state or city level for unpermitted decks under this height, but larger structures, even if low, still need to follow structural safety guidelines, like proper joist spacing and stable footings.

Anything exceeding 30 inches in height or with complex structural features, such as multiple levels, cantilevered sections, or roof attachments, requires a permit. Local inspectors check footings, joist sizes, and connections to ensure decks in Bozeman can withstand Montana's seasonal freeze-thaw cycles, wind, and snow loads. ### What Is the 3/4/5 Rule for Decks?

The 3/4/5 rule is a simple method to square your deck layout. It comes from the Pythagorean theorem:

- Measure **3 units** along one side of your deck layout.

- Measure **4 units** along the adjacent side.

- Measure the diagonal between those two points, it should be **5 units**.

If the diagonal measures correctly, your deck is perfectly square. This is a small step with a huge impact, preventing uneven boards, misaligned railings, and costly adjustments once construction begins.
