### What Salary Typically Affords a $300,000 House?

Most buyers need an annual salary of 60,000 to 75,000 dollars to comfortably afford a 300,000 dollar house, assuming a fixed 30-year mortgage, reasonable property taxes, and a standard debt-to-income ratio. Lenders want your total monthly debts, including the mortgage, to stay near or below 36 percent of your gross monthly income.

For a 300,000 dollar home in Bozeman, the monthly mortgage payment (including taxes and insurance) often lands between 1,750 and 2,000 dollars, depending on the interest rate and down payment. With a 10 percent down payment, a buyer earning about 5,000 to 6,200 dollars per month will usually meet approval standards.

The exact salary depends on your credit score, interest rate, existing debts, and local costs. In Bozeman, where prices and taxes vary by neighborhood, buyers with [stable income and low revolving debt](/blog) have the strongest approval odds. The formula is simple: the fewer loans and credit payments you carry, the lower the income needed to qualify.
