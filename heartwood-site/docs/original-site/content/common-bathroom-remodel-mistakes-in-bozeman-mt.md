## What Should You Not Do When Remodeling a Bathroom?

The most common bathroom remodel mistakes fall into a few predictable categories: budgeting errors, poor design planning, and material missteps. Many homeowners assume $10,000 will cover structural changes, quality fixtures, and labor, but a mid-range bathroom remodel in Bozeman typically runs $12,000–$18,000. Underestimating that gap leads to cut corners and compromised results.

Another frequent mistake is overlooking functionality. Cramped layouts, insufficient storage, or poorly placed lighting can make even an expensive bathroom feel awkward and frustrating to use. Material choice matters just as much, selecting finishes that can't withstand Montana's humidity and freeze-thaw cycles leads to premature wear, warping, and discoloration.

Designers at Heartwood Craft also caution against trend-chasing. Overly bold colors or niche fixtures might feel fresh today but can make your bathroom look outdated within just a few years. And skipping professional guidance on plumbing, electrical work, or code compliance is one of the costliest mistakes of all, one that often turns a manageable project into an expensive fix.

**Don't underestimate your budget or timeline.** Always build in a 10–20% contingency for unexpected issues, especially in older Bozeman homes where surprises behind the walls are common.

**Don't ignore ventilation and lighting.** These are critical for both comfort and mold prevention during Bozeman's cold, humid winters. Proper ventilation is one of the most overlooked elements in any remodeling Bozeman MT project.

**Don't skip code compliance.** Plumbing and electrical mistakes aren't just costly to fix, they can be genuinely unsafe. Local building codes in Bozeman exist for good reason, and Heartwood Craft ensures every project meets them.

**Don't overload the space.** Bulky vanities or oversized furniture that restricts movement will make any bathroom feel smaller and less functional, regardless of how well it's designed.

## What Is the 30% Rule in Remodeling?

The 30% rule is a practical budgeting guideline that suggests homeowners spend no more than 30% of their total renovation budget on any single room. The goal is to maintain financial balance and protect your return on investment across the whole home.

For example, if your total Bozeman remodeling budget is $40,000, allocating around $12,000 to the bathroom keeps spending proportionate while leaving room to improve other areas of the home. This rule is especially useful for homeowners in Bozeman who are considering [multiple projects](/services/remodeling), a bathroom refresh now, a basement remodel Bozeman later, or even a new outdoor deck down the line. ## What Makes a Bathroom Look Outdated?

Even a structurally sound bathroom can feel dated if the wrong design choices were made. The most common culprits Heartwood Craft encounters in Bozeman remodeling projects include:

- Old or mismatched fixtures that don't share a consistent finish or style

- Small, low-quality tiles or bold patterns that were trendy decades ago but now feel heavy and dated

- Poor lighting, specifically, a single overhead fixture with no layered or accent lighting

- Overly bold color choices or clashing finishes that make the space feel chaotic

- Insufficient storage that leads to cluttered countertops and a space that feels smaller than it is

The fix doesn't always require a full bathroom remodel in Bozeman. In many cases, targeted upgrades, new hardware, updated lighting, a statement mirror, and a fresh neutral palette, can modernize a bathroom significantly without a full overhaul.
