## What a Bathroom Remodel Actually Involves

Most homeowners don't know what's behind their bathroom walls, and that's fine — that's my job. Here's what a typical full remodel looks like:

**Demo.** Everything comes out — tile, vanity, toilet, tub or shower, drywall down to studs. This is where I check for water damage, mold, and structural issues that need fixing before anything new goes in.

**Plumbing rough-in.** Moving or replacing supply lines, drain lines, and valve positions. If you're converting a tub to a walk-in shower, this is where the drain gets relocated.

**Electrical.** Updating wiring to code, adding GFCI outlets, recessed lighting, exhaust fan installation or upgrade. Bozeman code requires specific circuit requirements for bathrooms.

**Waterproofing.** This is the step most DIYers and cut-rate contractors skip, and it's the reason bathrooms fail. Kerdi membrane or equivalent on every wet surface. Properly sloped shower pan. Sealed corners and penetrations.

**Backer board and tile.** Cement board substrate, then tile installation — floors, shower walls, niches, accents. This is where the room gets its personality.

**Fixtures and vanity.** Toilet, faucets, showerhead, vanity, mirrors, towel bars, accessories. The space starts to look real.

**Paint and finish carpentry.** Trim, baseboards, door casing, final paint. The details that make it feel finished, not renovated.

A full gut remodel typically takes **4-6 weeks**. A refresh — keeping the layout, updating surfaces and fixtures — takes **2-3 weeks**. Timeline depends on material lead times, scope, and whether we find surprises behind the walls.
