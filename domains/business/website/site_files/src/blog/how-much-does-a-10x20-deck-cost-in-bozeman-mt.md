## How Much Does a 10x20 Deck Cost in Bozeman?
For a 10x20 deck, 200 square feet, realistic cost ranges in Bozeman by material are:

**Pressure-treated wood: $5,000–$7,000.** The most budget-accessible option for decks Bozeman homeowners want to build without a large upfront investment. Properly installed pressure-treated lumber handles structural demands well, but requires consistent sealing, staining, and maintenance to hold up through Montana winters. Without that upkeep, freeze-thaw cycling and snowmelt accelerate deterioration faster than most homeowners anticipate.

**Composite decking (Trex, TimberTech): $10,000–$15,000.** The most popular choice across Bozeman remodeling projects involving outdoor living, and the material Heartwood Craft recommends most often for long-term use. Composite delivers a wood-like aesthetic with dramatically lower maintenance requirements, strong resistance to moisture and UV degradation, and durability that holds up through Bozeman's demanding seasonal transitions.

**PVC decking: $12,000–$18,000.** Fully synthetic, with no wood fiber content, making it the most moisture-resistant decking material available. PVC performs exceptionally well in conditions where snow sits on the deck surface for extended periods and temperature swings are significant. Slightly higher upfront cost than composite, but minimal lifetime maintenance and maximum weather resistance.

These ranges cover materials and standard construction, basic railings included. Additional features that affect the final number include built-in seating, lighting, stairs, multi-level configurations, and permit fees through the City of Bozeman's ProjectDox system. Structural requirements specific to the site, frost-depth footings, ledger flashing, slope grading, also influence cost and are assessed as part of every Heartwood Craft deck consultation.

## What is The Average Cost of a 12x20 Deck?
Stepping up from a 10x20 to a 12x20 deck, from 200 to 240 square feet, adds roughly $1,000–$3,000 to the total project cost depending on material, with the additional 40 square feet often delivering more functional return than the cost difference suggests.

For reference, Bozeman cost ranges for a 12x20 deck:

**Pressure-treated wood: $6,000–$8,000** **Composite decking: $12,000–$18,000** **PVC decking: $15,000–$22,000**

For families planning to use the deck for dining and lounging simultaneously, or anyone who entertains more than occasionally, the 12x20 footprint tends to justify the marginal cost difference. For homeowners with tighter budgets or more modest outdoor living goals, the 10x20 delivers strong functional value without unnecessary scope. ## Is It Cheaper to Pour Concrete or Build a Deck?
Concrete patios typically cost $8–$12 per square foot installed, making them cheaper upfront than most decking options at comparable square footage. For a 200 square foot surface, that translates to roughly $1,600–$2,400, a significant cost difference from even the most budget-friendly pressure-treated deck.

But cost per square foot is where the concrete advantage ends. Decks Bozeman homeowners invest in offer capabilities that concrete simply can't match: elevation above grade, multi-level configurations for sloped lots, integrated drainage that handles Bozeman's snowmelt and rain more effectively than a flat concrete surface, and the design flexibility to add railings, lighting, built-in seating, and custom features that transform an outdoor space rather than just paving it.

Concrete is a practical solution for flat, at-grade surfaces where simplicity and low cost are the priorities. A deck is a better investment when the goal is an outdoor living space that integrates with the home, handles Bozeman's terrain and climate intelligently, and adds measurable resale value alongside functional enjoyment. For most Bozeman remodeling projects involving outdoor living, the deck wins on total value delivered, and Heartwood Craft helps homeowners understand that comparison clearly before they commit to either path.

## How Much Does a 10 foot by 10 foot Deck Cost?
A 10x10 deck, 100 square feet, half the size of a 10x20, offers a useful lower reference point for homeowners evaluating size against budget.

Realistic Bozeman cost ranges for a 10x10 deck:

**Pressure-treated wood: $2,500–$3,500** **Composite decking: $5,000–$7,500** **PVC decking: $6,000–$9,000**

A 10x10 deck works well for intimate use, a bistro table for two, a pair of chairs, and a small container garden, but begins to feel limiting for family dining or any scenario where more than three or four people share the space at once. For most Bozeman households planning to get real daily use from their deck through the warmer months, a 10x20 footprint represents the functional minimum, and a 12x20 the practical sweet spot.
