## From First Call to Final Walkthrough

Most contractors keep you guessing. Here's exactly what happens when you work with Heartwood Craft.
### Step 0: What is your project? 

I know, its odd to have our process start with something YOU do, but the advice comes from experience. You don't have to have everything 
### Step 1: You Reach Out

Call, text, fill out the form, or use either our [bathroom cost calculator](/calculator/) or [deck project calculator](/deck-calculator). I call back — not a receptionist, not a sales team, me. We'll talk for about 15 minutes. I'll ask about your project, your timing, and whether you have a budget range in mind. If it sounds like a good fit, we schedule a site visit.

**What you get:** A real conversation with the person who'll actually do the work.

### Step 2: The Site Visit

I come to your home for a 45-minute visit. I measure everything, check for hidden conditions where I can (water damage, subfloor issues, plumbing access), and capture the full scope. I'll ask about how you use the space, what's frustrating you, and what you're hoping for. If you have a Pinterest board or design ideas, bring them — they help.

**What you get:** A thorough assessment from someone who knows what's behind the walls.

### Step 3: The 3D Rendering

If your project involves design decisions (layout changes, tile patterns, fixture placement), I build a 3D rendering of your space. You see exactly what your bathroom, basement, or room will look like before any demolition happens. We adjust until it's right. No guessing. No crossed fingers.

**What you get:** A virtual walkthrough of your finished space — before any work begins.

### Step 4: The Estimate

After the site visit, you get a detailed, itemized estimate. Every line item is visible: labor by trade, materials by item, allowances for selections you haven't made yet. If I've budgeted $1,500 for tile and you find something you love for $1,200, you keep the $300. The estimate is not a ballpark — it's a commitment.

**What you get:** Complete cost transparency. You see where every dollar goes.

### Step 5: Construction

Once you approve the estimate and sign the contract, we lock in a start date. You pay a 30% deposit, and I order materials. During construction, you get regular progress updates with photos — what's been done and what's coming next. If anything changes scope, I price it as a formal change order and get your approval before proceeding. No surprise costs. No radio silence.

**What you get:** Clear communication and a contractor who never disappears.

**Payment milestones:**
- 30% at contract signing
- 20% at rough-in complete
- 40% at substantial completion
- 10% at final walkthrough

### Step 6: The Walkthrough

When the work is done, we walk through everything together. I want you to look at every detail — tile joints, paint lines, hardware alignment, caulk, trim. If anything needs attention, write it down. Punch list items get resolved promptly.

**What you get:** A finished space you're genuinely proud of, with nothing left undone.

### Step 7: After the Project

You'll receive a post-project package with warranty information, care instructions, and my direct contact info. I check in at 6 months and again at 1 year. If something comes up, you call me — the same person who built it.

**What you get:** A contractor who stands behind his work long after the last invoice.
