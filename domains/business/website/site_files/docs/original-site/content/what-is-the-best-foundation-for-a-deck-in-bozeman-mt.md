## The Best Deck Foundation for Bozeman

The best foundation for a deck in Bozeman, MT is a **concrete footing or pier system installed below the local frost line**, which is typically **36 inches deep**. This depth prevents frost heave during winter freeze–thaw cycles, one of the most common causes of deck failure in southwest Montana.

Most code-compliant decks in Bozeman use **poured concrete footings with metal post bases** or **sonotube piers** tied into the structure. These systems distribute weight evenly, resist soil movement, and protect wood posts from moisture and rot. For larger or elevated decks, footings must also be sized correctly for load requirements, snow weight, and span distances, principles that apply equally to decks in Bozeman and basement remodel projects in Bozeman where structural integrity is paramount.

## What Is the 3/4/5 Rule for Decks?

The **3-4-5 rule** is a layout method used to ensure your deck is square:

- Measure **3 feet** along one side

- Measure **4 feet** along the adjacent side

- The diagonal between those points should be exactly **5 feet**

If it is, your layout is square. This simple step prevents framing problems, uneven decking, and railing alignment issues later on.
