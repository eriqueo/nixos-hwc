1\. Website Audit: iheartwoodcraft.com

I reviewed your entire site. Here's what's working, what's broken, and
what to fix. The site was built by an agency and is SEO-optimized with
proper location pages and service pages. That's a real asset. But there
are gaps that are costing you conversions.

What's Working Well

- **SEO structure is solid.** You have dedicated pages for Bozeman,
  Belgrade, Gallatin Gateway, plus service pages for bathroom, basement,
  remodeling, and decks. This is exactly how you rank for local
  searches.

- **Google reviews widget on homepage.** Rachel Dempsey, Ashley
  Phillips, and Susan McCauley reviews are visible. Social proof right
  where prospects see it.

- **Contact form is prominent.** Appears twice on the homepage. Phone
  number is in the header. Clear CTAs throughout.

- **Professional branding.** Clean logo, consistent color palette,
  professional photography. Doesn't look like a "I built this myself"
  site.

- **"3D design previews" mentioned in copy.** This is a real
  differentiator since you actually do this. But it's buried in
  paragraph copy. It should be a headline feature.

What's Broken or Missing

"Our Work" portfolio page is empty

**This is your single biggest conversion killer.** A homeowner lands on
your site, reads the copy, likes the reviews, clicks "Our Work" to see
examples... and finds nothing. They leave. A portfolio page with 4--6
before/after project galleries would dramatically increase the time on
site and conversion rate. Every completed bathroom, every deck, every
renovation should be documented here.

**Fix:** Upload 3--4 before/after galleries from your best projects
(Dempsey, Hendrix, Margulies). Include the 3D renderings where you have
them. Each gallery should have: project type, scope description, 3--5
photos (before, during, after), and client review if available. This is
a weekend project once you have WordPress admin access.

3D rendering capability is buried

The site mentions "3D design previews" in paragraph text on the general
remodeling and bathroom pages, but it's not a headline feature. Most
visitors will never notice it. This should be front and center because
it's the thing that separates you from every other contractor in
Bozeman.

**Fix:** Add a dedicated section on the homepage: "See Your Remodel
Before We Build It" with a 3D rendering example (before photo next to
rendering next to completed photo). This three-image story is your most
powerful sales tool. It should also be on the bathroom and basement
service pages.

Contact form may not be connected

You need to verify: when someone fills out the contact form, where does
the submission go? If it's emailing the agency and not you, leads are
dying in an inbox you don't control. Once you have WordPress admin
access, check the form plugin settings (likely WPForms or Contact Form
7) and make sure submissions go to eric@iheartwoodcraft.com. Ideally,
wire it to n8n: form submission → n8n webhook → creates JT customer +
Slack notification.

No analytics tracking

You mentioned wanting to set up your own analytics. Once you have admin
access, install Google Analytics 4 (GA4) and Google Search Console. GA4
tells you: how many people visit, where they come from, which pages they
look at, and where they drop off. Search Console tells you: what search
terms bring people to your site and what position you rank for. This
data is essential for knowing whether your SEO investment is working.

Blog exists but is likely empty

A blog with 2--4 posts about bathroom remodeling in Bozeman ("How much
does a bathroom remodel cost in Bozeman?", "5 signs your bathroom needs
a remodel", "What to expect during a bathroom remodel") would
significantly improve your SEO for long-tail keywords and give you
content to share on social media. Each post should be 800--1,200 words
with your project photos. Not a priority now, but it's the next SEO
lever after the portfolio is filled.

6\. Google LSA Optimization

Your LSA is running at \$210/week. Here's how to get the most out of
every dollar:

The LSA Ranking Factors (in order of importance)

20. **Review count and rating.** More reviews = higher ranking = more
    leads = lower cost per lead. This is why the review blitz was your
    first action item. Every review you add makes your LSA more
    effective.

21. **Response time.** Respond to every LSA lead within 5 minutes. The
    algorithm tracks this. Slow responders get deprioritized. If you
    can't answer, text back immediately.

22. **Budget.** Higher budget = more impressions. Your \$210/week is
    solid for Bozeman. Increase if leads are converting well.

23. **Service types.** Make sure ALL your services are listed. Every
    service is a keyword Google matches against.

24. **Photos.** 5--10 photos of your work on your LSA profile. Update
    quarterly.

25. **Business hours.** Wider hours = more visibility. Set to when you
    can realistically answer or return calls quickly.

LSA Lead Processing

Every LSA lead should follow this exact process:

26. Lead comes in → answer within 5 min (or text back within 5 min if
    you can't answer)

27. Run through Stage 2 (Qualify) questions on the call

28. Create JT customer account with Source = "Local Service"

29. Mark the lead as "Booked" in your LSA dashboard (this tells Google
    you're a good steward of their leads)

30. If it's a bad lead (wrong service, wrong area, spam) → dispute it
    for a credit

Monthly LSA Review

- Total leads this month vs. last month

- Cost per lead (total spend / total leads)

- Lead-to-visit conversion rate

- Leads disputed (and credited)

- Decision: increase budget, decrease budget, or hold?

Here's the complete optimization checklist:

One-Time Setup (do this weekend)

- **Business category:** Primary = "Remodeling contractor". Additional =
  "Bathroom remodeler", "General contractor", "Deck builder",
  "Carpenter".

- **Services listed:** Add every service you offer as a GBP service.
  Bathroom remodeling, shower remodeling, tile installation, deck
  building, custom carpentry, finish carpentry, drywall repair,
  painting, basement remodeling.

- **Business description:** 750 characters max. Include your key
  differentiators: bathroom specialist, 3D design previews, transparent
  pricing, local owner-operated. Include "Bozeman" and "bathroom
  remodel" naturally.

- **Photos:** Upload at least 10 photos. Before/afters, your
  truck/branding, you on a job site, detail shots. Google shows
  businesses with more photos more often.

Ongoing (weekly, as part of Monday Morning block)

- Post 1 photo per week as a GBP update. Caption: describe the work +
  location. Takes 2 minutes.

- Respond to every review within 24 hours. Thank positive reviews by
  name. Address negative reviews professionally and take the
  conversation offline.

- Answer any GBP Q&A that appears (Google lets anyone ask questions on
  your profile).
