## What Not to Say to a Contractor?

Direct, honest communication is the foundation of every successful remodeling Bozeman MT project. A few statements, however well-intentioned, consistently create friction, and sometimes set projects up for problems before they begin.

**"We're just looking for the cheapest option."** Low bids almost always reflect reduced scope, lower-quality materials, or skipped steps, and the savings rarely survive contact with the first hidden issue inside the walls. In Bozeman remodeling, value and cost are not the same thing.

**"We don't really need permits, right?"** In Bozeman, skipping permits for plumbing, electrical, or structural work creates real liability. Unpermitted work can block a future sale, require costly remediation, and, in the case of safety-related systems, create genuine risk. A reputable contractor will always insist on doing things correctly. Heartwood Craft handles every permit through Bozeman's ProjectDox system as a standard part of every project.

**"We'll figure out the details later."** Undefined scope is one of the most reliable predictors of budget overruns and timeline delays. The details are exactly what need to be established before construction begins, not during it.

**"We're flexible on budget."** Without a defined range, it's difficult to scope a project that actually serves your goals. A realistic budget range, not a firm ceiling, but an honest range, allows a contractor to propose work that aligns with what you're trying to achieve.

The better approach: be specific about your goals, clear about your budget range, and direct about your timeline expectations. That's the kind of conversation Heartwood Craft builds every Bozeman remodeling engagement around.

## What Are the Most Common Renovation Mistakes?

Understanding what goes wrong in renovation helps homeowners ask better questions, and choose better partners. The mistakes that cause the most stress and financial damage across remodeling Bozeman MT projects fall into consistent patterns.

**Choosing based on the lowest bid rather than the most detailed scope.** A low number without a clear scope breakdown is not a bargain, it's an unresolved question that will get answered during construction, usually at a higher cost than the original bid.

**Failing to verify licensing and insurance.** Montana contractor licensing and liability insurance protect homeowners when something unexpected happens. Ask for documentation, not assurances.

**Not understanding that labor drives 40–65% of remodeling costs.** Material costs are visible and easy to compare. Labor costs are where scope, quality, and timeline live. A contractor who can't explain their labor breakdown in detail is a contractor whose bid you can't evaluate accurately.

**Skipping the 10–20% contingency.** Hidden issues are a near-certainty in Bozeman's older housing stock. Water damage, outdated wiring, and substandard insulation behind walls don't appear on estimates, they appear during demolition. A proper contingency protects the project rather than derailing it.

**Ignoring energy code requirements.** A basement remodel in Bozeman that doesn't meet 2021 IECC insulation standards isn't just a code violation, it's a long-term energy performance problem that affects comfort and operating costs for years. Heartwood Craft builds insulation compliance into every basement and bathroom project scope from the start.

**Making major design changes mid-project.** Change orders after construction begins cost significantly more than decisions made during the design phase, in both money and time. Investing thoroughly in the planning stage prevents the expensive pivots that derail timelines and strain contractor relationships.

## What Questions Should I Ask a Remodeling Contractor?

For a structured, risk-aware approach to hiring a contractor for any Bozeman remodeling project, organize your questions across five areas:

**Licensing and Insurance** Are you fully licensed and insured in Montana? Can you provide current documentation for both?

**Permits and Code Compliance** Will you handle permits through Bozeman's ProjectDox system? How do you ensure compliance with 2021 IRC and IECC standards on a bathroom remodel in Bozeman, a basement remodel in Bozeman, or a deck project? Who is responsible if work fails inspection?

**Budget Clarity** Can you provide a line-item estimate rather than a lump sum? How are allowances handled when actual material costs differ from estimates? What typically causes cost overruns in projects like mine, and how do you manage them?

**Project Management** Who is on-site daily? How often will I receive progress updates, and through what channel? What are the projected start and completion dates, and what factors could affect them?

**Contingency Planning** How do you handle unexpected structural or moisture issues discovered during demolition? What contingency percentage do you recommend for a project of this scope, and why?

Detailed, documented answers to these questions separate contractors worth hiring from those worth passing on. At Heartwood Craft, these questions are part of every initial consultation, because they protect both the homeowner and the integrity of the project.

## What to Bring When Meeting With Contractor for the First Time?

Preparation makes the first meeting more productive and helps a contractor provide accurate, relevant guidance from the start. When meeting with Heartwood Craft or any Bozeman remodeling contractor for the first time, bring:

- **Photos of the current space**, especially any visible problem areas like water staining, damaged tile, or outdated fixtures

- **A clear must-have versus nice-to-have list**, knowing your priorities helps align scope to budget immediately

- **A realistic budget range**, not a number you're hiding, but an honest range that allows for meaningful scoping

- **Inspiration photos**, even rough visual references help contractors understand your aesthetic direction faster than descriptions alone

- **Any known issues**, leaks, drafts, past repairs, or areas you've had concerns about

For bathroom projects specifically, clarify upfront whether the scope is cosmetic, mid-range, or structural. That single distinction immediately aligns expectations around timeline, budget, and contractor coordination requirements.
