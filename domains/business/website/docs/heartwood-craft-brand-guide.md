# Heartwood Craft — Brand Identity & Voice Guide

## Purpose

This document defines who Heartwood Craft is, how it sounds, and what it never says. It's the reference for all content — website copy, ad campaigns, landing pages, social posts, email, and any AI-generated content. When in doubt, check this doc.

---

## The Company

**Heartwood Craft** is a remodeling company based in Bozeman, Montana, serving the Gallatin Valley (Bozeman, Belgrade, Gallatin Gateway). Founded and run by Eric. Specializes in bathroom remodeling, decks and exterior work, custom carpentry, and general remodeling.

The name comes from heartwood — the dense, structural core of a tree. It's the part that holds everything up. That's the brand metaphor: substance over flash, structure over shortcuts.

---

## Brand Position

Heartwood Craft occupies the space between the cheap handyman who ghosts you and the bloated general contractor who sends a project manager you've never met. The positioning is:

- **Premium, not luxury.** Quality work at fair prices. Not the cheapest, not the most expensive. The right price for work done properly.
- **Process-driven, not personality-driven.** The differentiator is a reliable, repeatable process — not charm, not hustle, not promises. The work speaks.
- **Selective, not desperate.** Heartwood Craft has enough work. The website and ads should feel like a confident craftsman making himself available, not a contractor chasing leads.
- **Local expertise, not generic contractor.** Bozeman-specific knowledge matters: freeze-thaw cycles, local codes, Gallatin County permitting, Montana build seasons, local material suppliers.

---

## Core Values

These are the actual values that show up in how work gets done — not marketing slogans:

- **Do it right the first time.** No cutting corners. If something needs to be redone, it gets redone before the client ever sees it.
- **Transparent process.** Clients know what's happening, what it costs, and why. No surprises. Clear scope, honest timelines, real numbers.
- **Respect the client's time and money.** Show up when you say you will. Charge fairly. Don't waste their Saturday on a sales pitch.
- **Craftsmanship is in the details.** The things nobody sees — waterproofing behind tile, proper flashing on a deck ledger, the right fasteners for Montana weather — that's where quality lives.

## Voice & Tone

### How we sound

- **Confident, not cocky.** State facts. Don't oversell. "We build decks designed for Montana winters" not "We build the BEST decks in Bozeman!"
- **Direct, not salesy.** Short sentences. Say what you mean. No filler words, no marketing fluff, no exclamation points.
- **Knowledgeable, not condescending.** Share expertise in a way that helps people make decisions. Don't talk down. Don't use jargon without context.
- **Warm, not corporate.** This is a real person doing real work. The tone should feel like talking to a skilled neighbor who happens to be a contractor — not a company website.
- **Understated, not flashy.** Let the work and the process do the talking. If something is good, you don't need to say it's amazing.

### Sentence-level guidance

- Write in second person ("you") when talking to clients, not third person ("our clients").
- Favor active voice. "We waterproof every shower pan" not "Every shower pan is waterproofed."
- Keep paragraphs short. 2–3 sentences max on the web.
- No superlatives unless backed by a specific fact. Not "the best bathroom remodeler in Bozeman" — instead, describe what makes the work good.
- Avoid clichés: "go above and beyond," "your satisfaction is our priority," "we treat your home like our own," "no job too big or small."

## What We Never Say

These are hard rules. They apply everywhere — website, ads, landing pages, emails, social, calculators, forms.

### Never say "free estimate" or "free consultation"

Eric charges for estimates on larger jobs. This is a feature — it filters for serious clients and funds thorough planning. Charging for the estimate means you get a real estimate, not a ballpark guess designed to win a bid.

**Instead say:** "Request a consultation," "Tell us about your project," "Schedule a site visit," "Start a conversation."

### Never make specific time-bound promises

"Call you back within the hour," "estimate within 48 hours," "we'll be there tomorrow." These create obligations that may not be met and sound desperate.

**Instead say:** "We'll be in touch," "Eric will reach out at a time that works for you," or just don't mention timing at all.

### Never position as desperate for work

No urgency language aimed at us getting the lead. No "limited availability," no "book now before we're full," no "call us anytime day or night."

**Appropriate urgency** is about the client's situation: "Montana's deck season is May through October — starting the conversation now gives you the best shot at a summer build."

### Never highlight being a solo operator (outside the About page)

The About page can be personal. Service pages, the homepage, and landing pages should let the work speak without "I'm Eric and I'm a one-person company" energy. Use "we" on main pages.

### Never promise features that aren't universal

3D design previews are available but not every project needs them. Daily photo updates are unrealistic to promise on every job. Don't advertise these as universal features.

**Instead:** Mention them as things that happen when appropriate. "On full remodels, we build a 3D rendering so you can see the space before demo day."

### Never use generic AI marketing language

- "Your dream bathroom awaits"
- "Transform your space"
- "We go above and beyond"
- "Experience the difference"
- "Your vision, our expertise"
- "Exceeding expectations since..."

If it sounds like it came from a template, cut it.

## Visual Identity

- **Primary color:** Copper (#cf995f) — warm, natural, craft-oriented
- **Background:** Warm cream (#E8E1D5 on site, #faf8f5 on light surfaces)
- **Text:** Charcoal (#23282d)
- **Typography:** DM Sans (body), Playfair Display (headings)
- **Photography:** Real project photos only. No stock photography. No AI-generated images. Show actual Heartwood Craft work in Bozeman homes.
- **Logo:** Heartwood wordmark. Used in header and brand contexts. No tagline in the logo.

## Audience

The typical Heartwood Craft client is:

- A homeowner in the Gallatin Valley (Bozeman, Belgrade, Gallatin Gateway)
- Owns a home built in the 1970s–2000s that needs updating, OR a newer home that needs custom work
- Values quality and reliability over the lowest price
- Has been burned by a contractor before (or has heard horror stories) and wants someone trustworthy
- Is willing to pay fairly for work done right — they're not shopping for the cheapest bid
- Often found through Google search, word of mouth, or a neighbor's recommendation

### What they care about (in order)

1. Will this person actually show up and do what they say?
2. Will the work be done right?
3. What will it cost and are there going to be surprises?
4. How long will it take?
5. Will my house be a disaster during construction?

The website, ads, and all content should address these concerns in this priority order.

## Content Patterns That Work

### For service pages

Lead with the client's problem. Then describe how the process solves it. End with a clear next step. Don't lead with "we" — lead with "you."

**Good:** "Your bathroom is 20 years old and it shows. The tile is cracked, the layout wastes space, and every morning is a compromise."

**Bad:** "At Heartwood Craft, we specialize in creating beautiful bathroom transformations for Bozeman homeowners."

### For the homepage

The homepage is a routing page — help people find what they need fast. Services grid, clear navigation, minimal text. Don't try to tell your whole story on the homepage.

### For landing pages

One goal per page. Match the ad headline. Strip the navigation. Short form, prominent CTA. Testimonials for social proof. Trust signals (licensed, insured, local). No distractions.

### For ads

Lead with the client's pain or desire, not with the company name. Be specific to Bozeman. Mention the service. Include a clear action.

**Good:** "Bathroom remodel in Bozeman? See your new space in 3D before demo day."

**Bad:** "Heartwood Craft — Bozeman's Premier Remodeling Company. Call Today!"

## Form & CTA Language

### Form headings

- "Tell Us About Your Project"
- "Start the Conversation"
- "Describe What You're Looking For"

### CTA buttons

- "Request a Consultation"
- "Schedule a Site Visit"
- "Get In Touch"
- "Tell Us About Your Project"

### CTA supporting text

- "No pressure. Just a conversation about what's possible."
- "We'll reach out to learn more about your project and find a time that works."

### Never for CTAs

- "Get Your Free Estimate"
- "Schedule My Free Estimate"
- "Book Now"
- "Claim Your Free Consultation"
- Any use of the word "free" in connection with services
